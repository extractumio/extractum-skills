## Warnings Section

```markdown
## Platforms to Avoid

| Platform | URL | Reason to Avoid |
|----------|-----|-----------------|
| [Name] | [URL] | [Specific reason: "Pay-to-play disguised as free listing" / "Very low DA, could hurt SEO" / "Strict self-promo rules, high ban risk" / "Abandoned/inactive since 2023"] |
```

Categories of warnings:
- **Pay-to-play traps** — directories that appear free but require payment for any meaningful visibility
- **SEO-harmful directories** — low-quality or spammy directories that could trigger Google penalties
- **High ban-risk communities** — forums with strict self-promotion rules where premature posting leads to account bans
- **Inactive/dead platforms** — sites that appear in search results but are no longer maintained or have negligible traffic
- **Vanity metrics traps** — platforms that show high numbers but deliver no real traffic or engagement

## Uncertainty Markers

Use these markers consistently throughout the output:

- **Verified** — Platform confirmed active, submission page found, URL tested
- **Likely active** — Platform appears in recent search results but submission page not directly verified
- **Unverified** — Platform mentioned in community discussions but could not be independently confirmed; flag these entries

Mark unverified entries with a warning symbol in the Evidence/Notes column.

## Composite Priority Scoring

When ranking entries, weight these factors:

| Factor | Weight | Score Range |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Speed to result | 25% | 1-5 (5 = impact within 24h) |
| Cost | 20% | 1-5 (5 = completely free) |
| Audience relevance | 25% | 1-5 (5 = exact target audience) |
| Evidence it works | 15% | 1-5 (5 = multiple competitors present + community validated) |
| SEO value | 10% | 1-5 (5 = dofollow from DR 70+) |
| Effort required | 5% | 1-5 (5 = 5-minute submission) |

**Composite score = weighted average.** Entries scoring 4.0+ go in Tier 1, 3.0-3.9 in Tier 2, below 3.0 in Tier 3.

You do not need to show the numeric scores in the final output — use them internally for ranking.
